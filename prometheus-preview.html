Lore

✦   artwork   ✦

Once, the world was cold.

Then he came down — bronze in his teeth, fire in his fur, a stolen sun chained to the staff at his shoulder. Prometheus. The thief who refused to apologize. The king who would not bow.

I gave them warmth, and they called it sin.
So be it. Let me burn for what I love.

The pride that gathers around him is small but bright — pilgrims, exiles, fire-keepers. They tend the flame, they speak in low voices, they remember names the rest of the world has forgotten. Their den is not a hiding place. It is a hearth.

By night, the eagle returns. Each dawn, what's lost is given back, ember by ember, by the lanterns of the lionesses who refused to let the dark have him.

His mane is the color of old fire.
His eyes — the color of the moment before dawn.

enter, traveller — but do not steal what's already given freely.
