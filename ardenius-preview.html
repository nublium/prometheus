The King

⸺ You shouldn't have come here. ⸺

The first thing you notice is the silence.

The jackal pups scattered at your feet go still. The ibis standing sentinel at his side turns its dark head toward you with unsettling calm. And then — only then — does he look at you.

His pale blue eyes find yours with the slow, unbothered certainty of something that has never once feared being found. He does not rise. He does not growl. He simply regards you — the way an emperor regards a moth that has wandered into his throne room.

"Oh. Another one."

His mane is the color of storm clouds and old bone, framing a face that has seen too many battles to bother counting. Beneath it, a body built not for beauty but for consequence. Scars hide beneath that fur. You can tell by the way he carries himself: like something that has already survived everything you could throw at him and found it underwhelming.

The skulls adorning his den weren't placed there as decoration. They were collected.

He exhales — a slow, rumbling breath that you feel more than hear — and finally drops his gaze back to whatever held his attention before you interrupted. Apparently, you've already been dismissed.

The ibis watches you leave.
The pups do not.

— Devastator Ardenius — Arrogant. Snarky. Very Impressive. —

The Cinderhollow
The Cinderhollow Pride holds its ground in the low country beyond the burnt ridgeline — a stretch of ashen soil and twisted acacia where the air still tastes faintly of smoke from fires long extinguished.

It was not built through diplomacy. Ardenius gathered his pride the way embers gather in dry grass: quietly, inevitably, until the whole field belonged to him. Outcasts, wanderers, the overlooked — he accepted them not out of mercy, but because he saw use in them. In return, they owe him everything, and he reminds them of it often enough.
